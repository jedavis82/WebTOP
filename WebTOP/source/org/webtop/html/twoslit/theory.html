Two Slit Photon: Theory


In this module a monochromatic plane wave of wavelength λ is normally incident upon two identical slits of width w that are separated by a distance d. The module simulates the arrival of photons on an observation screen a distance z behind the two slits. It is assumed that the screen is far enough from slits for Fraunhofer diffraction to occur.

Let (x,y) be the Cartesian coordinates of a point on the observation screen, and let P(x,y) be the probability that a photon arrives at this observation point. P(x,y) is proportional to the intensity predicted by classical wave theory, i.e.

P(x,y) = Csinc2[πxw/(λz)]cos2[πxd/(λz)]

where C is the constant of proportionality.
